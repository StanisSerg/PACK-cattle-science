# 01B. Distinctions: Cattle Science

> Distinctions are the foundation of the pack. They determine what counts as information, what practices are methods, and what terms are meaningful.

---

## D.001: Norm vs. Recommendation

| Aspect | Norm (Норма) | Recommendation (Рекомендация) |
|--------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Definition** | Quantitative requirement based on physiological needs | Suggested practice based on experience or context |
| **Basis** | Scientific research, metabolic studies | Field experience, economic considerations |
| **Precision** | Numeric (e.g., 18% crude protein) | Qualitative (e.g., "high quality forage") |
| **Mandatory** | Must be met for optimal performance | Adjustable based on circumstances |

**Example:**
- Norm: "Dry cow requires 0.35 MJ NEL/kg W^0.75"
- Recommendation: "Provide comfortable lying areas"

**Test:** Can you measure compliance numerically? → Norm : Recommendation

## D.002: Physiological Period vs. Management Period

| Aspect | Physiological Period | Management Period |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Definition** | Biological stage of lactation/gestation | Operational grouping for feeding/management |
| **Duration** | Variable (depends on individual) | Fixed (convenience-based) |
| **Basis** | Hormonal, metabolic changes | Practical farm logistics |
| **Examples** | Early lactation (0-100 DIM) | "Fresh cows" (first 21 days) |

**Importance:** Mismanagement occurs when management periods don't match physiological needs.

## D.003: Input vs. Output Parameter

| Aspect | Input Parameter | Output Parameter |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Definition** | What is provided to the animal | What is produced by the animal |
| **Control** | Directly controllable by manager | Result of inputs and animal status |
| **Examples** | Feed amount, protein content | Milk yield, fertility rate |
| **Use** | Ration formulation | Performance evaluation |

**Key insight:** You manage inputs; you measure outputs. Confusing them leads to wrong decisions.

---

## D.004: Individual vs. Herd Level

| Aspect | Individual Level | Herd Level |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Focus** | Single animal performance | Group averages and distributions |
| **Precision** | High (specific to cow) | Lower (averages hide variation) |
| **Management** | Individual treatment | Group protocols |
| **Economics** | Not economically viable for all | Cost-effective at scale |

**Decision rule:** Individual when cost of error > cost of individual attention; Herd when scale matters.

---

## D.005: Biological Potential vs. Economic Optimum

| Aspect | Biological Potential | Economic Optimum |
|--------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Definition** | Maximum possible performance | Most profitable performance |
| **Goal** | Maximize output | Maximize margin (output - cost) |
| **Inputs** | Unlimited (theoretical) | Constrained by cost |
| **Example** | 50+ kg milk/day (possible) | 35-40 kg milk/day (profitable) |

**Critical distinction:** Pushing to biological potential often reduces profitability.

---

## D.006: Acute vs. Chronic Condition

| Aspect | Acute Condition | Chronic Condition |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Onset** | Sudden | Gradual |
| **Duration** | Hours to days | Weeks to months |
| **Detection** | Obvious symptoms | Subtle performance decline |
| **Example** | Milk fever (acute) | Subclinical ketosis (chronic) |
| **Economic impact** | Immediate, visible | Long-term, hidden but larger |

**Management implication:** Chronic conditions often cost more due to delayed detection.

---

## D.007: Model vs. Reality

| Aspect | Model | Reality |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Definition** | Simplified representation | Complex, variable system |
| **Purpose** | Prediction, decision support | Actual farm operation |
| **Precision** | Approximate | Exact (but unknown) |
| **Use** | "What if" scenarios | Implementation |

**Warning:** Models are useful but dangerous when confused with reality. Always validate with field data.

---

## D.008: Justified Decision vs. Opinion

| Aspect | Justified Decision | Opinion |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **Definition** | Claim backed by explicit source and decision criteria | Personal preference or intuition without reviewable basis |
| **Source** | Research, norm, farm historical data, or framed hypothesis with verification plan | «I feel», «I always do it this way», «it works» |
| **Testability** | Can be disputed on evidence; success/failure can be measured | Cannot be falsified; any outcome confirms it |
| **Transferability** | Can be taught, reviewed, and replicated | Locked in individual experience |

**Decision rule:** A technologist may propose a change, but must be able to answer:
1. What is the source? (research / norm / farm data / hypothesis)
2. What outcome do you predict?
3. When and how will we measure it?
4. What is the criterion to rollback?

If at least one of these is missing, the claim is an opinion, not a justified decision.
