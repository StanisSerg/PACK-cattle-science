# 01D. Domain Principle: Tacit Expertise vs Explicit Knowledge Transfer

## Principle

> **The main bottleneck in cattle science practice is not the absence of good technologists, but the absence of a system for transferring their knowledge. The industry relies on tacit expertise that takes years to replicate, because the knowledge is locked in individual intuition rather than explicit, reviewable, and teachable forms.**

## Context

In dairy cattle operations, experienced practitioners (veterinarians, nutritionists, herd managers, feed consultants) often possess strong intuitive systems thinking:
- they sense when a ration is "off";
- they recognize early signs of metabolic disorders;
- they know how to adapt protocols to local conditions;
- they understand trade-offs between productivity, health, and economics.

However, this expertise remains **tacit** (personal, context-bound, hard to articulate) and is transmitted mainly through:
- long-term apprenticeship;
- personal observation;
- trial and error;
- informal storytelling.

There is no shared language, no explicit methods, no auditable evidence graphs, and no scalable training system.

## Why This Matters

| Problem | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| **Tacit knowledge** | Cannot be transferred quickly; each new specialist must relearn through years of practice. |
| **No explicit methods** | Decisions cannot be reviewed, improved, or disputed on merit. |
| **No evidence trail** | It is unclear why a decision was made or whether it worked. |
| **Person-dependent operations** | If the expert leaves, the knowledge leaves with them. |
| **Slow scaling** | Good practices spread farm-by-farm, year-by-year, instead of being deployable. |

## Relation to FPF / PACK

This principle is one of the core motivations for applying FPF to cattle science:

| Tacit Form | FPF / PACK Form |
|------------|-----------------|
| «The technologist feels the ration is wrong» | Pre-articulation cue → Description → Hypothesis → Dynamics |
| «This vet is good because experienced» | Role + MethodDescription + Work record + Evidence Graph |
| «Every farm is unique, methods don't transfer» | Bounded Context + Bridge + Scope Mechanism (USM) |
| «Training takes 3 years of practice» | Captured methods, rubrics, checklists, and verification protocols |

**FPF does not replace the technologist.** It makes the technologist's knowledge:
- **explicit** — visible and discussable;
- **reviewable** — open to critique and improvement;
- **teachable** — transferable without full personal replication;
- **auditable** — traceable from claim to evidence to outcome.

## Implications for PACK-Cattle-Science

1. **Methods must be first-class artifacts.** A method is not «what Ivan does well» — it is a documented, scoped, reviewable MethodDescription.
2. **Every claim needs a source.** A recommendation must be backed by evidence, model, or explicit authority — not just seniority.
3. **Context must be explicit.** A practice that works in one farm must carry its bounded context; transfer to another farm requires a bridge, not blind copying.
4. **Work must be recorded.** What was planned, who did it, by what method, and with what result — this is the loop that turns intuition into organizational learning.

## Examples

### Example 1: Early ketosis detection
- **Tacit:** «I can tell by the cow's eyes and smell.»
- **Explicit FPF form:** Pre-articulation cue → protocol for measuring BHB + DIM context + threshold rule + vet confirmation Work record.

### Example 2: Ration adaptation after feed change
- **Tacit:** «You have to wait two weeks before judging a new ration.»
- **Explicit FPF form:** Dynamics claim with stated lag, measurement schedule, characteristic (milk fat %, rumination time), and scope (early/late lactation).

### Example 3: Disagreement between nutritionist and veterinarian
- **Tacit:** «They always argue; the owner decides by gut feeling.»
- **Explicit FPF form:** Conflict framed as different bounded contexts (feeding vs health) with explicit claims, evidence, and decision criteria.

## Validation Criteria

A knowledge artifact in this pack should reduce dependence on individual tacit expertise by making at least one of the following explicit:
1. The claim it makes.
2. The context in which it applies.
3. The method for applying it.
4. The evidence for trusting it.
5. The work record for verifying it.
